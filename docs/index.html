How SJU Radar works

SJU Radar sweeps live round-trip fares once a day (around 8:00 AM in Puerto Rico) through the Ignav API, and records each route’s history. A route’s typical price is its 120-day median; a fare becomes a signal when it sits meaningfully below that range, near its recent floor, or under your configured target.

Statuses compare each fare with your own targets: Below target when the fare beats the threshold you set, Near floor when it sits within 10% of the lowest price recently observed, and Above target otherwise. Alert tiers come from each route’s percentiles — below the 25th, below the 10th, or unusually low overall (a possible mistake fare).

Alerts fire on Telegram when a fare beats its target or sets a new floor (24-hour cooldown). Every link opens live bookable prices — airline-direct when Ignav can build the link, otherwise a live Google Flights query. The fare at your checkout screen is the only guarantee. Split builds use separate tickets and carry their own risk. Targets live in config.yaml; strategy and heuristics in the companion playbook.
